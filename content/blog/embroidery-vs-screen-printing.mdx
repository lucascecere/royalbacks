I run an embroidery shop. I'm going to tell you when screen printing is the better call.

## When Embroidery Wins

Embroidery is the right choice when the item needs to last. Work shirts, polos, hats, jackets — anything that gets washed repeatedly and worn in the field. Thread doesn't crack, peel, or fade the way plastisol ink does after 50 washes.

Embroidery also reads as premium. There's a tactile quality to a raised logo on a hat or a left-chest crest on a polo that printing can't replicate. If your brand positioning matters, embroidery signals quality.

**Best for**: Corporate uniforms, work shirts, hats, fleece, outerwear, anything that gets heavy use.

## When Screen Printing Wins

Screen printing handles large, complex designs better than embroidery. A full-color photographic design across the back of a t-shirt? Print it. Embroidery struggles with photographic gradients and very fine detail below about 4mm.

Printing is also cheaper at high quantities for simple designs. If you need 500 event t-shirts with a basic one-color logo, screen printing will beat embroidery on cost every time.

**Best for**: Event t-shirts, promotional items, simple bold graphics, large runs (200+).

## The Hybrid Approach

Most teams we work with end up using both. Embroidered polos and hats for staff and coaches. Printed t-shirts for events and fans. You don't have to choose one.

## Our Honest Recommendation

If you're asking because you want the item to look good for more than a season, embroider it. If you're asking because you need 300 shirts for a 5K next month, print them.
