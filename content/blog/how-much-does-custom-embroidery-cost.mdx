Custom embroidery pricing in 2026 depends on three factors: the number of stitches in your design, the quantity of items, and the garment type. Here's what to actually expect.

## Stitch Count Is Everything

Most embroidery shops quote by the thousand stitches (K stitches). A simple left-chest logo on a polo might run 4,000–8,000 stitches. A full back design on a jacket can hit 20,000+. At Royal Backs, a standard left-chest logo on 24 polos typically runs $12–$18 per piece including the garment.

## Quantity Breaks Matter

Embroidery has real setup costs — digitizing your artwork, hooping the first piece, running the machine through color changes. Those costs spread across more items as quantity goes up. A run of 12 hats costs more per piece than 48 hats.

## What You're Actually Paying For

- **Digitizing**: Converting your logo to an embroidery file (one-time fee, $25–$75 for a standard logo)
- **Thread**: Commercial polyester thread, 40-weight is standard
- **Machine time**: Our Tajima machines run at around 800 stitches per minute
- **Garment**: We source from S&S Activewear and alphabroder — same wholesale channels as the big guys
