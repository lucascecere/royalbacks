A crew in matching embroidered shirts looks like a company. A crew in random t-shirts looks like a side hustle. That's the whole argument.

We do work uniforms for contractors across Massachusetts — electricians, plumbers, landscapers, general contractors, HVAC techs, and anyone else who shows up at a job site and wants their company to look right.

## Why Embroidery for Work Gear

Embroidery holds up. Screen-printed logos peel, crack, and fade after repeated industrial washing. A well-digitized embroidered logo on a Carhartt or Port Authority polo will outlast the shirt itself. For gear that gets washed regularly and worked hard, embroidery is the better investment.

## What Contractors Order

**Work shirts**: Polo shirts or button-downs with a left-chest company logo. Clean, professional, instantly identifiable.

**Work jackets**: Softshell and insulated jackets for Massachusetts winters. Logo on left chest, optional name on right chest.

**Hats**: Company hats for crews, foremen, and owners. Snapback or fitted, structured or unstructured.

**High-vis gear**: We embroider on ANSI-compliant safety vests and shirts — logo placement on non-reflective areas.
